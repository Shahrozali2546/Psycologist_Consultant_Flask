Treatment of Depression

Depression is a frequent mental health problem that results in a lingering melancholy as well as changes in thinking, sleeping, eating, and acting. There are numerous varieties. Treatment involves either talk therapy or medication. It is critical to get medical attention as soon as you have symptoms.

About 80% to 90% of depressed individuals who seek treatment eventually have a positive response.

Options for Treatment

  • Psychotherapy: Talking with a mental health professional. CBT is the most popular form.
  • Medication: Antidepressants that help alter brain chemistry. Side effects may occur but often subside.
  • Brain Stimulation Therapy: Includes ECT, TMS, and VNS for severe depression or psychosis.
  • Complementary Therapies: Acupuncture, massage, hypnosis, biofeedback for mild depression.

Additional Home Remedies

  • ✔️ Regular exercise
  • ✔️ Healthy sleep habits
  • ✔️ Balanced diet
  • ✔️ Avoiding alcohol
  • ✔️ Staying socially connected

Therapies for Depression

  • Psychodynamic Therapy
  • Behavioral Activation (BA)
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
  • Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

Benefits of Online Counselling for Depression

  • Better awareness about symptoms and causes
  • Learn coping skills and improve self-esteem
  • Reduce severity of symptoms
  • Improve decision-making and confidence
  • Improve communication & relationships
  • Prevent future relapse with long-lasting outcomes
  • Safe, judgment-free environment
  • New perspectives and address underlying issues
Note: Online counseling is a personalized process and benefits may vary from person to person.
