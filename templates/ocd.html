What is OCD?

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) is a mental health condition where individuals experience persistent, distressing thoughts (called obsessions) and feel compelled to perform repetitive behaviors or rituals (called compulsions) in an attempt to relieve their anxiety.

These behaviors often interfere with daily functioning and can occur multiple times a day. Although the symptoms vary, they usually fall into recognizable patterns. People may experience symptoms from more than one category, which can evolve over time.

Treatment Options for OCD

  1. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Includes ERP to face fears and reduce compulsions.
  2. Medications: SSRIs, TCAs, or SNRIs to manage symptoms.
  3. Psychiatric Hospitalization: For severe or crisis cases.
  4. Other Therapies: ACT, DBT to support emotional and behavioral change.
  5. Supportive Interventions: Family therapy, psychoeducation, or support groups.
  6. Lifestyle Adjustments: Sleep, exercise, nutrition, and relaxation techniques.
