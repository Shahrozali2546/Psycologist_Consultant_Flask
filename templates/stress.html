What is Stress?

Stress is a natural physical, emotional, and behavioral response to internal or external pressure...

Types of Stress

  • Acute Stress : A short-term response to immediate challenges.
  • Chronic Stress : Long-term stress resulting from ongoing situations.
  • Episodic Acute Stress : Frequent episodes of acute stress.
  • Eustress : Positive stress that can motivate and improve performance.

Signs & Symptoms of Stress

  • Physical: Headaches, muscle tension, fatigue, sleep issues.
  • Emotional: Anxiety, irritability, sadness.
  • Cognitive: Difficulty concentrating, memory issues.
  • Behavioral: Overeating, smoking, substance use.
  • Social: Withdrawal, isolation.
  • Relationships: Conflicts with others.
  • Performance: Decreased productivity.

Causes of Stress

  • Work: Heavy workload, poor management.
  • Finances: Debt, unemployment.
  • Relationships: Conflicts with loved ones.
  • Health: Chronic illness, injuries.
  • Life Changes: Moving, marriage, loss.
  • Environment: Noise, pollution.
  • Personal: Perfectionism, negative mindset.

Challenges of Stress

  • High blood pressure, heart disease
  • Anxiety, depression
  • Relationship conflicts
  • Work and academic issues
  • Substance abuse
  • Cognitive impairments
  • Family and sleep issues

Treatment and Management of Stress

  • Relaxation Techniques: Breathing, muscle relaxation
  • Mindfulness Therapies: MBSR, MBCT
  • Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
  • Exercise, healthy routine
  • Time management, social support
  • Medication if prescribed

Therapeutic Approaches

  • Anger Management
  • Psychosynthesis
  • Person-Centered Therapy
  • Transactional Analysis
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
  • Interpersonal Therapy (IPT)

Benefits of Online Counseling

  • Self-awareness & coping skills
  • Improved emotional health
  • Reduced physical symptoms
  • Better relationships & communication
  • Boosted confidence & productivity
